When I'm not writing code, you'll find me exploring new technologies, experimenting with data analysis in Jupyter notebooks, or refining my understanding of system design and distributed computing.

Philosophy

Principles I
build by

01

Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication

Great code reads like prose. I obsess over naming, structure, and clarity — because the most elegant solution is the one your future self (and your teammates) can understand at 2 AM.

02

Ship early, iterate always

Perfection is the enemy of progress. I believe in getting a solid v1 out the door, gathering real feedback, and iterating relentlessly. The best features come from real usage data.

03

Engineer for humans first

Whether it's an API, a UI component, or an internal tool — the end user is always human. I design developer experiences with the same care I put into user-facing products.

04

Leave it better than you found it

Every PR is an opportunity to improve the codebase. Refactor adjacent code, add missing tests, improve docs. Small improvements compound into transformative change over time.
